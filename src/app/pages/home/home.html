The Island

An island that forgot it was this close to a capital

Lidingö has a way of slowing the clock. Cross the bridge from Stockholm and the traffic gives way to pine, rock, and water — one of the greenest, most sought-after addresses in the archipelago, and one of its best-kept secrets. Story Living sits where the parkland meets the shoreline, close enough to watch the ferries pass and far enough to forget the city ever existed.

Then the old wooden tram, the Lidingöbanan, rattles you back into the middle of it all in ten minutes flat. Escape and commute, somehow, in the same short ride.

The Neighborhood

Quiet enough to think, close enough to work

Lidingö isn't a suburb you pass through — it's a destination companies are starting to choose on purpose. No traffic noise, no tourists, just trail paths, water, and everything a working life actually needs within walking distance.

Trail Paths & Nature Reserves

The 440-hectare Långängen-Elfvik nature reserve and the Kottlasjön loop are minutes away — old-growth forest, lake trails, and views over the archipelago for the walking meeting or the morning reset. Lidingö is also home to Lidingöloppet, the world's largest cross-country race, run right through these woods every year.

Gym, On Your Doorstep

SATS Lidingö sits in the same waterfront Dalénum district as Story Living — a proper gym, not a hotel treadmill, close enough to visit before the first call of the day. Fitness24Seven in Baggeby is open around the clock for the team that keeps odd hours.

Restaurants for Client Dinners

From the harbor tables at Hamnen Kök & Bar to the everyday ease of Kvarters Lidingö and the kitchens of 450 Gradi and Restaurang Toscana, there's a real choice of places to take a client, a colleague, or just yourself after a long day — all a short walk or drive from home.
